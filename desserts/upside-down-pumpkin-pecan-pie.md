## Ingredients

**For Pecan Layer:**
- 3/4 cup (165g) brown sugar, packed
- 1 tablespoon (14g) butter, melted
- 1 1/2 cups (170g) pecans, chopped

**For Pumpkin Filling:**
- 1/2 of 12 oz can (6 oz/170g) evaporated milk
- 1 1/2 cups (370g) canned pumpkin pie mix (Libby's or similar)
- 2 large eggs
- 1 unbaked 9-inch pie crust

**For Serving:**
- 3/4 cup (180ml) whipping cream, whipped

## Instructions

1. **Prepare pecan layer:** Line a piece of wax paper in the bottom of the pie crust. Sprinkle brown sugar, melted butter, and chopped pecans on the wax paper. Place a strip of aluminum foil on outer edge of crust to prevent over-browning.

2. **Prepare filling:** In large bowl, whisk together pumpkin pie mix, evaporated milk, and eggs until smooth.

3. **Add filling:** Carefully pour pumpkin filling over the pecan layer. Bake in preheated 425°F (220°C) oven for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 350°F (175°C) and bake 40 more minutes, or until knife inserted in center comes out clean.

4. **Cool and invert:** Cool for 4 minutes to loosen edges slightly. Run a knife around the edge if needed. Carefully invert onto serving plate and remove pie pan and wax paper. The pecan layer will now be on top. Serve warm or at room temperature with whipped cream.

## Notes

- **Upside-down technique:** The wax paper is crucial for this recipe - it allows the pie to release cleanly when inverted, with the pecans forming a beautiful caramelized top layer.
- **Doneness test:** Knife inserted in center should come out clean. The filling will still have a slight jiggle but will set as it cools.
- **Inversion timing:** Don't wait too long to invert - 4 minutes is ideal. If it cools too much, the sugar layer will stick to the pan.
- **Foil shield:** The aluminum foil strip protects the crust edges from over-browning during the longer bake time.
- **Storage:** Refrigerate leftovers covered for up to 3 days.
- **Serving suggestion:** Best served slightly warm or at room temperature with freshly whipped cream.
