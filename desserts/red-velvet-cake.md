## Ingredients

**Cake:**
- 2½ cups (310g) all-purpose flour
- ½ cup (65g) cornstarch
- 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
- 1 cup (240ml) buttermilk (or substitute: 1 tbsp lemon juice + milk to 1 cup line)
- 1½ cups (360ml) vegetable oil
- 3 large eggs, at room temperature
- 1 tsp (5g) baking soda
- 1 tsp (5ml) white vinegar
- 1 tsp (5ml) vanilla extract
- 2 tbsp (10g) unsweetened cocoa powder
- ½ tsp (2.5g) salt
- 1 oz (28g) red food coloring (liquid or gel)

**Cream Cheese Frosting:**
- 8 oz (225g) cream cheese, at room temperature
- ½ cup (113g) unsalted butter (1 stick), at room temperature
- 3 cups (360g) powdered sugar, sifted
- 1 tsp (5ml) vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt

## Instructions

### Make Buttermilk Substitute (if needed)

1. Measure 1 tbsp lemon juice into measuring cup. Add milk to 1-cup line. Stir and let sit 5-10 minutes.

### Make the Cake

**Prep:**
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
2. Grease and lightly flour two 9-inch (23cm) round cake pans.

**Mix dry ingredients:**
3. In large bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder, cornstarch, and salt. Set aside.

**Mix wet ingredients:**
4. In another large bowl, combine sugar and vegetable oil. Beat with electric mixer on medium speed until well combined.
5. Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.

**Add vinegar and coloring:**
6. In small bowl, mix baking soda and white vinegar. It will fizz; once fizzing stops, add to sugar-oil mixture.
7. Stir in vanilla extract and red food coloring.

**Combine:**
8. Add one-third of flour mixture to wet mixture, stirring until just combined.
9. Add half the buttermilk, stir until just combined.
10. Repeat: remaining flour in two additions, alternating with remaining buttermilk. Don't overmix.

**Bake:**
11. Divide batter evenly between prepared pans. Smooth tops with spatula.
12. Bake 25-30 minutes until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.

**Cool:**
13. Cool in pans 10 minutes, then transfer to wire rack to cool completely.

### Make Cream Cheese Frosting

1. Beat butter until soft and creamy.
2. Add cream cheese and beat until well combined and very smooth.
3. Gradually add powdered sugar, one cup at a time, beating well after each addition.
4. Stir in vanilla extract and pinch of salt until well combined.

### Assemble

1. Place one cake layer on serving plate.
2. Spread generous layer of cream cheese frosting on top.
3. Place second cake layer on top of frosting.
4. Spread frosting on top and down sides until fully covered.
5. Decorate with fresh berries if desired.

## Notes

- Room temperature eggs and dairy ensure smooth batter and frosting
- Don't overmix cake batter or texture will be dense instead of tender
- Baking soda + vinegar reaction helps create the cake's signature light texture
- Make sure cakes are completely cool before frosting or it will melt
- Can be made a day ahead; store frosted cake covered in refrigerator
- Bring to room temp 30 minutes before serving for best flavor
