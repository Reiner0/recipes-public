## Ingredients

- 1 onion, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, sliced
- 1 pound boneless pork shoulder, trimmed and cut into cubes
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin (regular or smoked)
- ½ teaspoon ancho chile powder
- ½ teaspoon chipotle chile powder
- ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 1 cup water
- 2 tablespoons coconut oil

### For Serving
- Guacamole
- Salsa
- Sour cream
- Cotija cheese
- Corn tortillas (optional)

## Instructions

1. **Season pork:** In the Instant Pot, combine the onion and garlic and toss to combine. Place the pork in a large bowl. In a small bowl, combine the salt, pepper, oregano, cumin, ancho chile powder, chipotle chile powder, and paprika and stir to thoroughly combine. Sprinkle the seasonings over the pork. Drizzle with the lemon juice. Toss until everything is well coated.

2. **Add to pot:** Place the pork on top of the onions and garlic in the Instant Pot. Pour the water into the large bowl and swirl to rinse out the last of the spices; pour the water over the pork.

3. **Pressure cook:** Secure the lid on the pot. Close the pressure-release valve. Select MANUAL and set the pot at HIGH pressure for 35 minutes. At the end of the cooking time, allow the pot to sit undisturbed until the pressure has released naturally. Transfer the pork to a shallow dish or large bowl.

4. **Reduce sauce:** Select SAUTE. Allow the sauce to reduce while you finish the dish.

5. **Crisp the pork:** Using two forks, shred the meat. Heat the coconut oil in a large cast-iron pan over high heat. Lay the shredded meat in a flat, even layer in the pan and let it sit undisturbed. When it starts to brown, flip it and keep cooking until well crisped. Add a little of the concentrated sauce to the skillet. The skillet should be hot enough that most of the sauce just evaporates, leaving behind the flavor. Continue to add all of the sauce, in small portions at a time, to flavor the meat. Select CANCEL.

6. **Serve:** Serve the pork with guacamole, salsa, sour cream, cotija, and/or tortillas, if desired.

## Notes

This Mexican pulled pork (carnitas) is perfect for tacos, tamales, or even low-carb snacking with avocados and sour cream. The crisping step in the cast-iron pan adds incredible texture and concentrates the flavors.
