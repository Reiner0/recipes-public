## Ingredients

- 1 cup dried pinto beans
- 2 cups cool water
- 1 (14.5-ounce) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes, undrained
- 1 cup finely chopped white onions
- ½ cup finely chopped fresh cilantro
- ½ cup finely chopped green bell pepper
- 6 slices bacon, chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1-2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1-2 teaspoons salt

## Instructions

1. **Soak beans:** Place the pinto beans in a medium bowl. Cover with hot water. Soak for 1 hour; drain.

2. **Combine ingredients:** Place the pinto beans in the Instant Pot. Add the cool water, tomatoes and their juices, onions, cilantro, bell pepper, bacon, garlic, cumin, and salt.

3. **Pressure cook:** Secure the lid on the pot. Close the pressure-release valve. Select MANUAL and set the pot at HIGH pressure for 30 minutes. At the end of the cooking time, allow the pot to sit undisturbed for 10 minutes, then release any remaining pressure.

4. **Thicken (optional):** If desired, use an immersion blender to puree some of the beans to thicken the broth.

## Notes

While salt pork is traditionally used in this recipe, bacon is used here as it's more readily available. These beans, along with Mexican rice, are staple side dishes for Mexican food. You can either leave the beans whole or use an immersion blender to puree half of them for a thicker consistency.
