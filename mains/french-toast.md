## Ingredients

- 4 large eggs
- 1 cup (240ml) milk
- 2 tsp (10ml) vanilla extract
- 1 tbsp (12g) granulated sugar
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- 8 slices challah bread
- Butter for frying (and toasting if necessary)

**For Serving:**
- Powdered sugar
- Maple syrup
- Optional: fresh berries, whipped cream

## Instructions

1. **Toast bread (if fresh):** If challah bread is fresh, preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly toast bread for about 10 minutes until just slightly crispy. This helps it absorb egg mixture without becoming too soggy. Let cool before using.

2. **Make custard:** In large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, vanilla extract, sugar, and cinnamon until well combined.

3. **Preheat skillet:** Preheat large non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat.

4. **Butter skillet:** Lightly coat skillet or griddle with butter.

5. **Soak and cook:** Soak each slice of toasted challah bread in egg mixture, making sure both sides are well coated. Do this one at a time, just before you're ready to cook. Place soaked slices onto skillet and cook until golden brown on each side, about 2-3 minutes per side.

6. **Serve:** Serve immediately. Top with dusting of powdered sugar and drizzle of maple syrup. Add fresh berries or whipped cream if desired.

## Notes

- Pre-toasting fresh bread prevents soggy French toast by reducing moisture content
- Day-old bread works great and doesn't need pre-toasting
- Cook over medium heat—too hot and outside burns before inside cooks through
- Don't oversoak bread or it will fall apart
- Keep finished slices warm in 200°F oven while cooking remaining batches
- Challah's eggy, slightly sweet flavor makes superior French toast, but brioche also works well
