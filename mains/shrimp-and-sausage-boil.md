## Ingredients

- 12 ounces smoked sausage, cut into 4 pieces each
- 3 ears sweet corn, cut into thirds
- 12 baby red potatoes, halved
- 1 tablespoon Louisiana-style shrimp and crab boil seasoning
- 4 tablespoons (½ stick) salted butter
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- Juice of ½ lemon
- ½ teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
- ½ teaspoon Cajun seasoning
- ½ teaspoon lemon-pepper seasoning
- 3-5 shakes hot sauce
- 1 pound shrimp (21 to 25 count), peeled and deveined
- Lemon slices, for serving

## Instructions

1. **Combine ingredients:** In the Instant Pot, combine the sausage, corn, and potatoes. Add enough water to cover. Add the shrimp and crab boil seasoning.

2. **Pressure cook:** Secure the lid on the pot. Close the pressure-release valve. Select MANUAL and set the pot at HIGH pressure for 4 minutes.

3. **Make butter sauce:** Meanwhile, in a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and cook, stirring continuously, until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes. Add the lemon juice, Old Bay, Cajun seasoning, lemon-pepper seasoning, and hot sauce. Stir until warmed through and keep warm.

4. **Check doneness:** At the end of the cooking time, quick release the pressure. Open the lid carefully and check to ensure the potatoes are cooked. If they are not done, boil them for a few minutes using the SAUTE setting.

5. **Add shrimp:** Gently stir in the shrimp. As soon as the shrimp turn pink, drizzle everything with the spiced garlic-butter sauce. Add the lemon slices to the pot. Stir gently until everything is well coated.

## Notes

Call it a Low Country boil, Frogmore stew, or Cajun shrimp boil—or just call it delicious. Most of the flavor in this dish comes from the richly spiced butter, so make a little extra if you'd like, and enjoy!

This is one of the most pinned recipes for two reasons: shrimp done right, and heavenly, buttery, garlicky, spicy sauce.
