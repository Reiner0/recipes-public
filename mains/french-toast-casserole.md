## Ingredients

**Casserole:**
- 1 cup brown sugar
- 1/2 cup (113g) unsalted butter
- 1 (8-ounce) loaf crusty French bread, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 cups (480ml) milk
- 6 large eggs
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

**Topping:**
- 1 tablespoon brown sugar, or to taste
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon, or to taste

## Instructions

1. **Prep:** Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

2. **Make caramel base:** Stir 1 cup brown sugar and butter together in a saucepan over medium-low heat until butter is melted and sugar has dissolved, 2 to 4 minutes. Pour into the prepared baking dish.

3. **Layer bread:** Scatter French bread pieces over the caramel in a 1½- to 2-inch layer.

4. **Make custard and soak:** Whisk milk, eggs, and vanilla together until well combined. Pour over bread, then press bread down with a spatula so it absorbs the liquid. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate 8 hours or overnight.

5. **Prep to bake:** Remove from refrigerator and discard plastic wrap. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).

6. **Add topping:** Mix 1 tablespoon brown sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl; sprinkle evenly over the top.

7. **Bake:** Bake until golden brown and set in the center, 50 to 60 minutes. Cut into squares and serve with the caramel from the bottom of the pan spooned on top.

## Notes

- Quick-bake option: 450°F (230°C) for about 30 minutes — cover loosely with foil if the top browns too quickly.
- Fruit add-ins work great: blueberries or sliced apples are particularly good.
- Finish with a dusting of powdered sugar or extra cinnamon before serving.
- Storage: leftovers keep covered in the fridge for up to 3 days; reheat in a 325°F (165°C) oven.
