## Ingredients

### For the Gyros
- 1 small red onion, coarsely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 pounds ground lamb
- 2 teaspoons ground marjoram
- 2 teaspoons dried rosemary
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2 teaspoons black pepper
- 2 cups water

### For Serving
- 4 pitas, cut in half
- 2 large tomatoes, quartered
- 1 cup sliced cucumber
- ½ cup thinly sliced red onion
- Tzatziki (see recipe)

## Instructions

1. **Process aromatics:** In the bowl of a food processor, combine the onion and garlic. Process until finely chopped.

2. **Mix meat:** In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, combine the ground lamb and the onion mixture. Add the marjoram, rosemary, oregano, salt, and pepper. Beat the mixture until it is sticky. Don't skip this step, or you'll have a hamburger with seasoning—not a smooth gyro loaf.

3. **Prepare pot:** Pour the water into the bottom of the Instant Pot. Set a steamer rack in the pot. Place a piece of aluminum foil on the rack.

4. **Shape loaves:** Divide the meat mixture into two equal pieces and shape them into loaves. Place the loaves on the foil, leaving enough space on the sides for steam to circulate.

5. **Pressure cook:** Secure the lid on the pot. Close the pressure-release valve. Select MANUAL and set the pot at HIGH pressure for 15 minutes. At the end of the cooking time, allow the pot to sit undisturbed until the pressure has released naturally. Remove the meat loaves from the pot and place them on a platter. Cover with foil, then place a heavy object on top to compress them (I use a cast-iron skillet). Let stand for 30 minutes.

6. **Serve:** Thinly slice the meat loaves and serve with pita, tomatoes, cucumber, sliced onion, and tzatziki.

## Notes

The pressure cooker version is much more moist and juicy than the oven version. Yes, the meat will look like a pale lump when it emerges from the pressure cooker, but you won't care once you taste it. This recipe is definitely a fan favorite.
