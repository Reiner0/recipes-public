## Ingredients

- ½ cup dried shiitake mushrooms
- 2 cups hot water (for soaking)
- 2 cups glutinous rice
- 4 links lap xuong (Chinese sausage), sliced
- 2 cups cool water or chicken broth
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons oyster sauce
- 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon salt
- Sliced scallions, for serving

## Instructions

1. **Soak mushrooms:** In a medium bowl, soak the dried mushrooms in the hot water for 20 minutes; drain and chop.

2. **Combine ingredients:** In the Instant Pot, combine the mushrooms, rice, sausage, cool water, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and salt. Stir to combine.

3. **Pressure cook:** Secure the lid on the pot. Close the pressure-release valve. Select MANUAL and set the pot at HIGH pressure for 10 minutes. At the end of the cooking time, allow the pot to sit undisturbed for 10 minutes, then release any remaining pressure.

4. **Rest rice:** Stir to combine the ingredients and let stand, covered, for a few minutes to allow all the water to be absorbed, if necessary.

5. **Serve:** Divide the rice mixture among 4 serving plates. Top each serving with sliced scallions.

## Notes

Traditional recipes ask you to soak glutinous rice, but this isn't necessary with an Instant Pot. This recipe cooks up perfectly without the soaking you'd need on the stovetop.

This re-creates almost exactly the lo mai gai you'd get as dim sum, but without the hassle of wrapping the rice in hard-to-find lotus leaves. You can add bite-size pieces of chicken as well, if you prefer.

Chinese sausage keeps forever and can also be used in stir-fries, omelets, spring rolls, fried rice, and many other dishes.
