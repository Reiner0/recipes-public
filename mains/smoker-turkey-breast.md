## Ingredients

**Brine:**
- 8 quarts (7.5L) water
- 1 cup (280g) kosher salt
- 1 cup (200g) brown sugar
- 2 tbsp peppercorns
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed (or 6 tsp minced garlic)
- 4 bay leaves

**Turkey:**
- 6-8 lbs (2.7-3.6kg) bone-in turkey breast
- Salt
- Pepper
- Olive oil

## Instructions

### Brining (12-18 hours ahead)

1. **Make brine:** Put salt and brown sugar in large container. Add about 2 quarts hot water and mix until salt and sugar are dissolved.

2. **Add aromatics:** Add peppercorns, garlic, and bay leaves.

3. **Fill with cold water:** Fill up to 8 quarts total with cold water.

4. **Brine turkey:** Add turkey, ensuring it's mostly submerged. Refrigerate for 12-18 hours.

5. **Prep for smoking:** Pat turkey completely dry and let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes before seasoning.

### Smoking

1. **Preheat smoker** to 225°F.

2. **Season turkey:** Make sure surface is completely dry. Lightly oil turkey, then season with salt and pepper. Go light on salt since it's already brined.

3. **Insert thermometer:** Place meat thermometer in thickest part of breast.

4. **Low smoke:** Put turkey directly on grate and smoke at 225°F until it reaches about 145°F internal temp (3-4 hours).

5. **High finish:** At 145°F, raise smoker temp to 350°F.

6. **Rest:** Pull turkey from smoker at 167°F internal temp. Tent loosely with foil and let rest for 15 minutes.

7. **Serve:** Carve and serve.

## Notes

- **Don't over-brine:** Longer than 24 hours will make the texture hammy
- **Dry surface is critical:** Pat dry and let rest before smoking to ensure good smoke adhesion and crispy skin
- **Two-stage cooking:** Low temp (225°F) gets smoke flavor, high temp (350°F) crisps skin
- Temperature carries over during rest—pulling at 167°F will result in ~170°F after resting
