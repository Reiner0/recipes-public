## Ingredients

**Pork:**
- 1 pork butt (shoulder or Boston butt, 8-10 lbs)

**Dry Rub:**
- ½ cup black pepper
- ½ cup salt
- ¼ cup paprika
- ¼ cup onion powder
- ¼ cup garlic powder

**Other:**
- Olive oil

## Instructions

1. **Prep pork:** Trim any overhanging fat.

2. **Season:** Rub pork with olive oil. Apply dry rub generously all over.

3. **Start smoking:** Place in smoker at 275°F with fat side up. If fat cap isn't rendering after a few hours, flip it over.

4. **Wrap:** After 4-5 hours when fat cap splits, wrap tightly in foil and add water pan to smoker.

5. **Increase temp:** After 1 hour wrapped, increase smoker temp to 300°F.

6. **Finish:** Continue cooking until internal temp reaches 200-205°F.

7. **Rest:** Let rest for at least 30 minutes before pulling apart.

## Notes

- Total cook time is 6-7 hours for an 8-10 lb pork butt
- The "stall" around 160-170°F is normal—wrapping helps push through it
- Fat cap splitting is visual cue that collagen is breaking down
- Target internal temp of 200-205°F ensures meat is pull-apart tender
- Can rest in cooler wrapped in towels for up to 2 hours
- Shred with forks or bear claws, discarding large fat pieces
