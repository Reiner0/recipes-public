## Ingredients

- 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
- 3 cups water
- 2 (14-ounce) cans creamed corn
- 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- ½ cup water (optional)
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch (optional)
- 1-2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil
- ½ cup chopped scallions (green part only)

## Instructions

1. **Combine ingredients:** In the Instant Pot, combine the chicken, water, creamed corn, vinegar, soy sauce, salt, and white pepper.

2. **Pressure cook:** Secure the lid on the pot. Close the pressure-release valve. Select MANUAL and set the pot at HIGH pressure for 10 minutes. At the end of the cooking time, allow the pot to sit undisturbed for 10 minutes, then release any remaining pressure.

3. **Thicken soup (optional):** If you are using cornstarch to thicken the mixture, in a small bowl, stir together the water and cornstarch to make a slurry. Select SAUTE on the Instant Pot. When the soup boils, stir in the slurry and cook, stirring continuously, until soup is slightly thickened, 1 to 2 minutes. Select CANCEL.

4. **Serve:** Stir in the sesame oil. Divide the soup among six bowls. Garnish with the scallions and serve.

## Notes

This is an Indian-Chinese soup that is a staple in most Chinese restaurants in India. It's what I call "Chinese food even the Chinese have never had." When Hakka workers migrated to India, they were born out of homesickness but used locally available ingredients. This has spawned a whole subcuisine.

This is a fast, comforting soup that is kid-friendly while having plenty of flavor for the grown-ups. You can also double the recipe and serve it as a meal instead.

You can also thicken the soup by slowly drizzling beaten raw eggs into the soup instead of the cornstarch, or you can add both.
