## Ingredients

- Vegetable oil, for greasing
- 1 (8.5-ounce) package corn muffin mix
- 1 (14-ounce) can creamed corn
- 1 (4.5-ounce) can chopped mild green chiles, or ½ cup chopped poblano pepper
- ½ cup whole milk
- 2½ cups water, divided
- ½ teaspoon unflavored powdered gelatin or agar-agar

### Optional Topping
- Poblanos & Corn in Cream (if available)

## Instructions

1. **Prepare pan:** Generously grease a 6-inch springform pan with oil.

2. **Mix batter:** In a large bowl, combine the corn muffin mix, creamed corn, chiles, milk, ½ cup of the water, and the gelatin. Stir well to combine. Pour the batter into the prepared pan. Cover the top with aluminum foil.

3. **Set up steamer:** Pour the remaining 2 cups water into the Instant Pot. Place a steamer rack in the pot. Set the pan on the rack.

4. **Pressure cook:** Secure the lid on the pot. Close the pressure-release valve. Select MANUAL and set the pot at HIGH pressure for 25 minutes. At the end of the cooking time, allow the pot to sit undisturbed until the pressure has released naturally.

5. **Cool:** Set the pudding on a wire rack to cool to room temperature. Run a knife around the edges to separate the pudding from the pan. Carefully remove the springform ring.

6. **Serve:** Cut the pudding into wedges. Top with Poblanos & Corn in Cream, if desired.

## Notes

Sweet and savory, creamy, and slightly chewy from the corn—this pudding is a perfect side dish.

Gelatin helps the custard set and hold together, but you could use 2 large eggs instead.

Corn muffin mix is used for convenience, especially as it has so few additives.
